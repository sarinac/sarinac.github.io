My Story

I found inspiration in my career

I made this website to showcase my journey combining creativity and data with the goal of collaborating with others and inspiring many more. I’ll try my best to describe the design process, coding challenges, and takeaways from each project.

I found purpose for pairing data with design

Choosing math

I started out as a bioengineering major at UC Berkeley because I watched the movie GATTACA in my high school AP biology class and thought it would be cool to design people. I imagined magically tweaking someone’s kid to grant their cosmetic preferences - green-colored eyes or a perfect height. And Berkeley accepted me so… Anyways, in my first semester, I realized that I didn’t want to jump straight into grad school, nor did I want to work in a lab.

After asking many upperclassmen about their majors within the College of Engineering, I decided that statistics was the most intriguing and applicable to industry and everyday life. I switched to Industrial Engineering and Operations Research, and while taking my first probability class I saw my future became more colorful… more beautiful… It was like lifting a raincloud from over my head.

Including design

In my junior year I took a semester off to do a co-op at The Walt Disney Company, specifically at Disneyland behind Space Mountain. Doing Workforce Management in the theme parks, I discovered that my interest in art was incredibly effective at work when I created a presentation to showcase some projects from my 6 months. Since my department consisted heavily of those with people-skills rather than technical-skills, the challenge was to talk without b o r i n g.

Having self-taught much of the Adobe Creative Cloud suite while doing a lot of publicity on campus, I decided to up my presentation game by sneaking in graphic design. I spent a lot of time storyboarding, making custom icons, and iterating with my manager who was very supportive of me trying something new. During my presentation, the people who were normally quiet, non-technical, or not even involved in my work were proactively chiming in and answering questions for me during Q&A. It was such a big hit that they asked me to share with more people.

It was a no-brainer that art was a special skill to have in the data realm, and I wanted to surpass my potential. From then on, I always thought about how I would go about visualizing complex topics to my audience. I believe that art influences how people perceive the results of my work even when the show is the often at the last part of the project.
